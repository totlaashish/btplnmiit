Subscribed Electronic Resources

Guidelines for Fair Use of E-Resources

IEEE All-Society Periodicals Package (ASPP) & Proceeding Order Plan (POP)(Multi user license)

ASPP & POP provides access to the IEEE core collection of engineering, electronics, and computer science of 160 IEEE periodicals and electronics, and computer science of 160 IEEE periodicals and 125 core conference proceedings.
http://ieeexplore.ieee.org/

Science Direct

Elsevier Science and Academic Press journals can be accessed through Science Direct, which is a web based database of full text articles pertaining to Science, Technology and Medicine (STM). Science Direct offers a rich electronic environment for research journals, bibliographic databases and reference works. The library has the subscription to selective titles of EE, Electronics, SE, IT and CS collections only.
http://www.sciencedirect.com/

The ACM Digital Library

The ACM Digital Library incorporates digital versions of works published by ACM since its inception. Nearly half a century of pioneering concepts and fundamental research have been digitized and indexed in a variety of ways in this resource. The major components of the resource is an enhanced version of the ACM Digital Library plus an extended bibliographic database, consisting initially of more than a quarter-million citations of core works in computing. These works are of all types (journals, proceedings, books, technical reports, theses, among others), and from all the major publishers in the discipline.
http://portal.acm.org/portal.cfm

ASME (The American Society of Mechanical Engineers)

ASME is the premier professional membership organization for more than 127,000 mechanical engineers and associated members worldwide. ASME offers access to 26 ejournals Package Journals plus backfile to 2000.
http://asmedigitalcollection.asme.org

JSTOR

JSTOR is a digital library of more than 2,000 academic journals, 20,000 books, and two million primary sources. It provides access to the highest-quality academic journals, and primary sources in the humanities, social sciences, and sciences. Users can access JSTOR content by going directly to www.jstor.org and selecting Search or Browse from the menu bar.
www.jstor.org

APS (American Physical Society)

APS publishes 14 leading peer-reviewed journals, the collection includes 9 Journals, 4 Open Access Journal, and 1 Archive.
https://journals.aps.org/about

Project Muse

Project MUSE is a leading provider of digital humanities and social sciences content; since 1995, its electronic journal collections have supported a wide array of research needs at academic, public, special, and school libraries worldwide. Library subscribed to Humanities and Social Sciences collections only.
www.muse.jhu.edu

MathSciNet (Mathematical Reviews)

MathSciNet is an electronic & easily searchable database of reviews, abstracts and bibliographic information for much of the mathematical sciences literature. Bibliographic data from retro-digitized articles dates back to the early 1800s. Over 100000 new items are added each year, most of them classified according to the Mathematics Subject Classification.
http://www.ams.org/mathscinet/

E Books (Elsevier)

The Library has access to 111 books.
http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/bookbshsrw/all/subscribed/

Scopus

Scopus is the largest abstract and citation database of peer-reviewed literature: scientific journals, books and conference proceedings.
https://www.scopus.com/

World eBook Library

World eBook Library is the World’s largest collection of Primary Source eBooks consists of 40 Lacs eBooks and millions of Journal Articles with Unlimited Access and Downloading.
http://community.worldlibrary.in/CollectionCatalog.aspx?CatalogId=2

South Asia Archive

The South Asia Archive is a specialist digital platform providing global electronic access to culturally and historically significant literary material produced from within - and about - the South Asian region.
It is not merely a repository, but a vehicle for targeted research, and one which has been intelligently structured to ensure efficient content discovery.The South Asia Archive provides online access to millions of pages of rare primary and secondary sources from across the social sciences and humanities.
www.southasiaarchive.com
